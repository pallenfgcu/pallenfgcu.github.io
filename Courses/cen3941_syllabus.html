CEN 3941 Internship in Software Engineering

print[↑]
Florida Gulf Coast University
U.A. Whitaker College of Engineering
Department of Computing & Software Engineering

Course Catalog Description:

An experiential learning exercise that allows for practical application of knowledge acquired from the software engineering curriculum or program. Applicable to both unpaid and paid internships held within the current academic year or subsequent summer. This work is usually completed off campus and the student will have a qualified supervisor at the site as well as a faculty mentor who will jointly evaluate the student's progress Students are expected to have an internship or offer letter prior to the first day of class. Students without active internships will not be allowed to continue in the class.

Prerequisites:

COP 3530 for level Undergraduate with minimum grade of C

Required Material

Textbook:

None

Learning Outcomes:

  1. To integrate theory and practice, and to augment learning from the degree-program's curriculum.
  2. To develop communication and interpersonal skills pertaining to a realistic work environment.
  3. To acquire credible work experience and explore career paths.
  4. To fulfill contractual commitments and assigned job functions.
  5. To assess professional competency, conduct, development, and growth.
  6. To equip with ethics, processes, methods, and tools applicable in professional practice.

Instructional Methods:

The modality of course delivery is Online. Additionally, meetings may be arranged for group discussions or presentations.

Attendance:

None

Communication:

All course related communication should be via Canvas message. Each student is responsible for checking his/her FGCU email at least once a day. The instructor will respond to your emails within 24 hours except weekends and holidays.

Topics / Tentative Schedule:

  • Initial Assignment for CEN 3941: Internship in Software Engineering (Week 1)
  • Midterm Reflection on Internship Progress
  • Final Reflection on Internship Experience

Assessment:

There is no grace period for late submission of assignments. All assignments with due dates and times will be posted on Canvas. Alternate schedules can be arranged ahead of time with instructor approval.

Assignments:

There are 3 assignment submissions in Canvas that include reflections on your internship at the beginning, mid-semester, and end of semester. Read each assignment for requirements. Assignments are pass/fail.

Grading

Satisfactory or Unsatisfactory
